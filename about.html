Meet Marcus

I Know Exactly What You're Carrying.
I Carried It for 26 Years.

"You don't have a porn problem. You have an 8-year-old boy still standing in that kitchen, waiting for his dad to turn around and choose him. We're going to deal with that — and when we do, the behavior stops making sense."

My name is Marcus Jenkins. Senior Director of Finance at a mid-size logistics company. Husband to Taneesha for 14 years. Father to Marcus Jr., Amani, and Zion. I served as a deacon and men's ministry leader at a 2,000-member Atlanta church for six years. On Sunday mornings I preached that men could walk in sexual freedom. On Monday nights after Taneesha went to sleep, I was back on the same websites. For 26 years, that was the private architecture of my life — performing freedom, carrying shame, cycling back to the same sin, the same confessions, the same private promises.

In November 2018, Taneesha found my browser history. That night broke me open in the most necessary, devastating way. What followed wasn't another men's program or accountability cycle — it was a complete excavation of my theology, my nervous system, and my identity as a son. I sat with my own father wound for the first time. I grieved what I never received from my dad. I met the Father who had been running toward me the entire time. In 2021, something genuinely shifted. Not as a willpower victory. As a healing. I've been free since — not because I'm gritting my teeth, but because the root wound that drove the behavior has actually been healed.

I've since walked six men through this journey informally. All six remain free: Darius 14 months, Tre 11 months, Brandon 16 months, Jason 9 months. Marriages restored. Children being fathered well. Men showing up in their churches without the weight of a secret. That's why I'm here building this formally. The work is real. The freedom is real. And you are not too far gone.

— Marcus
