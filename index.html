My Story

I Used Porn for 26 Years. Got Free — Not Through Shame.

"The gospel I believed kept me trapped in a cycle of shame and relapse. The gospel I discovered — sonship, not slavery — set me free. The difference wasn't more effort. It was finally meeting the Father who'd been running toward me the whole time."

I'm Marcus Jenkins. Corporate finance director. Husband to Taneesha. Father to three. Former deacon and men's ministry leader at a 2,000-member Atlanta church for six years. And for all 26 of those years I was also using pornography. Every shame-based Christian program — Every Man's Battle, Covenant Eyes, Celebrate Recovery — gave me 30 clean days and then another relapse, another shame spiral, another private vow. It wasn't until a coach walked me back to an 8-year-old boy standing in the kitchen watching his dad pack his bags that everything finally changed. Grace, sonship theology, and nervous system healing did what accountability never could.

— Marcus
